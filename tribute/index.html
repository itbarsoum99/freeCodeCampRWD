Barack Obama

A timeline of Obama's presidency

  • November 5, 2008 - Barack Obama is elected president by defeating Senator John McCain with nearly 53 percent of the popular vote and 365 electoral votes (out of 538).
  • January 20, 2009 - Barack Obama is inaugurated as the 44th President of the United States. He becomes the first African American President in U.S. history.
  • February 17, 2009 - The American Recovery and Reinvestment Act is signed into law by President Obama while in Denver, Colorado.
  • October 9, 2009 - Obama is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for “his extraordinary efforts to strengthen international diplomacy and cooperation between peoples.”
  • March 20, 2010 - The Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, known as Obamacare, becomes law.
  • December 22, 2010 - Obama repeals the “Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell” policy, which allows people to openly be gay, lesbian, and bisexual while serving in the U.S. Armed Forces.
  • February 2, 2011 - Obama signs a nuclear arms treaty with Russia, called New START.
  • May 1, 2011 - Obama announces the death of Osama Bin Laden, who was the leader of Al-Qaeda.
  • November 6, 2012 - Obama defeats Republican Mitt Romney to win a second term in office with 332 electoral votes to 206 and 51 percent of the popular vote.
  • February 12, 2014 - Obama signs an executive order to increase the minimum wage from $7.25 to $10.10 for all workers on federal contracts.
  • July 21, 2014 - Obama signs an executive order to protect the rights of LGBT employees in the workforce.
  • November 30, 2015 - President Obama attends in the United Nations Climate Change Conference in Paris, France.
  • January 17, 2016 - Obama announces historic agreement with Iran to prevent the acquisition of nuclear weapons.
  • January 20, 2017 - President Obama concludes his last day in office.
